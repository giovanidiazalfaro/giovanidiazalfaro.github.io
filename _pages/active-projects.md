### Team Learning for Better Scientific Software

#### My Role (Project Contributor)

I'm part of a 12-person interdisciplinary team of researchers from universities and national laboratories across the U.S., addressing a core challenge in scientific software development: interdisciplinary teams are increasingly essential for tackling complex scientific problems, but synthesizing such varied expertise into effective collaborative learning remains genuinely difficult.

#### Why This Matters
Team learning has been identified as a key driver of performance and collaboration effectiveness in scientific teams, yet there's been limited infrastructure to support or evaluate it. This project works to build an open knowledge base and practical tools that help interdisciplinary teams learn more effectively while building scientific software.

#### Ongoing Work
The TL4BSSw group is currently developing a manuscript for submission to a computational science and engineering venue.
