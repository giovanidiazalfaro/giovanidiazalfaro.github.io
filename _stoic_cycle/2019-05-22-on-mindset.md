<p>     Our mindsets are a very fragile, yet imperative part of us. A mindset serves as the driving force for a multitude of factors in our daily life. Emotionally and behaviorally, we're extremely susceptible to influences stemming from our mindset. For example, let us say that you are having an absolutely terrible week. Friday comes around, and you wake up. Discontent, annoyed,  and dreading the day you are. This week has been absolute garbage, and you expect nothing more from today. You couldn't care any less that today is Friday, and you're not only dwelling on how bad the week has been, but also every minor inconvenience occurring throughout the day. And as a result, you are only making the day <em>that much worse</em>. Now go back, and attempt to change that start. You wake up on Friday, and although your week has been terrible, you refuse to let today be terrible as well. You take in every minor, or major inconvenience as what they are - minor or major inconveniences. And simultaneously, you remind yourself that their effect <em>on </em>you, is decided <em>by </em>you.  You have the choice between responding in a manner detrimental to your well-being, or responding in a manner of an understanding, patient, and realistic nature. And in doing so, not only does your Friday not "suck," but you can retrospectively alter your perspective and feelings toward the week's prior days. </p>

<p>     Simple, right? If only it actually were. In all honesty, it is pretty difficult, and not always pleasant. It is never easy for one to repeatedly react in a manner that is positive, and/or realistically beneficial. Part of the reasoning behind this, is the fact that acting in this manner isn't always pleasant. It's nice to not let an inconvenience such as a coffee spilling all over yourself get you down or annoyed, but it's not necessarily nice realizing that a person you have taken interest in, doesn't share that interest in you. But the thing is, reacting to both situations in a realistically beneficial manner is equally as beneficial; to your mindset, and your well-being. Value lies not only in doing so, but in the resulting mental effects. You begin to become presently aware of your accomplishments and progress by doing so, as well as the positive effects that result. Feelings and mindset are both extremely difficult to exercise any sort of control over, but not many truly rewarding skills are developed with ease. It takes practice, failure, and success to do so. But what can possibly be more valuable and/or rewarding than the ability to be in control of your mindset, mental faculties, and emotions? We all have the capability to choose how we interpret and respond to stimuli. The struggle, lies in not only the realization of that fact, but also in interpreting and responding to these stimuli in a manner that is beneficial to ourselves, even when it's not necessarily pleasant. </p>

<p>     Deliberate, focused, and persistent effort directed toward doing so can prove invaluable to each and every one of our lives, and cultivating this perspective on, and skill in having control over our mindset opens the doors to living a life in which you decide the outcomes. You accept the externals, strive to do as much as you can to cultivate beneficial atmospheres out of what you can control, and in turn live a life in which both happiness, and unhappiness are choices you not only must make, but must also be aware in doing so. And that, is a truly rewarding way to live. </p>
