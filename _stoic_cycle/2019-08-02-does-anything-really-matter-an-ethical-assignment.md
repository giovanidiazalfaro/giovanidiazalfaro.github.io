<p>     This semester, I took a course titled Advanced Ethics in Science and Technology. One of the assignments in this course, was to choose from a list of 39 topics, or to choose and get your own approved, and write a short 2-3 page paper responding to it. I chose to explore the prompt: "Does anything really matter?" I simply felt that I might as well post my response here as it seems to fit the mold quite well, and it serves as yet another sort of transcript of my own absurd and diverse mental processes. </p>

<p style="text-align:center;">Does anything really matter?</p>

<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Out of the 39 proposed topics given, I
felt inclined to choose this one due to the fact that although it may be the
simplest prompt in terms of literal understanding of the question, it also comes
off to me as the most daunting of questions to actually explore. And to do so, one
should tread lightly and meticulously, analyzing first the question and its
implications, and then one’s&nbsp;&nbsp; own
response. </p>

<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; First of all, how am I interpreting
this question? Anything, quite literally, is implying that we are considering
all things, with no reference to any in particular, no matter what. And in
terms of the word matter, quite simply we are taking it as meaning to be of
importance to some extent. This being the case, the question is asking if there
is, in a boundless scope, any single, or several things, that are of any semblance
of importance. This being the case, we have now created an analyzed basis off
of which to attempt to explore this philosophical question, to which I am
initially stating that I do not believe anything really matters. </p>

<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Personally, I have studied teachings
of existentialism, nihilism, stoicism, and other philosophical schools in the
past, both by choice, and by academic requirement. I have mentioned these three
specifically, because they prodigiously impact my current views on the matter
of, well, <em>mattering</em>. Existentialism
is a philosophical school that strives to emphasize the concept of existence,
specifically within the scope of free will. It maintains that existence itself
is inherently meaningless. This is an existential given, along with the fact
that meaning cannot be created nor discovered. But what they do also maintain,
is that each and every individual possesses complete free will, and if one so
chooses, they can accept this state of meaningless, and embrace it, conveying
the idea that although life is inherently meaningless, it does not have to be
unenjoyable as well. Nihilism on the other hand, is an extremist position that
also stresses the utter lack of meaning in life, as well as the fact that there
are no such things as values. In this case, we have those who convey a complete
disregard for anything at all, embodying the prompt in the sense that they
believe nothing matters, nor will anything ever matter. And then, there are the
stoics. Stoicism as a school of philosophy maintains that virtue matters above
all else. They very clearly define that which matters, with varying positive
and negative reasons for doing so. </p>

<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; These philosophical schools were
some of the few that served as my introduction into philosophical thinking and
analysis, and as stated prior, the three that I believe personally lend
themselves best to my exploration of this prompt. I at one point, prematurely
believed that Nihilism held the truth in its palms, more so out of a stubborn response
to my relinquishing of not my personal religious beliefs, but the traditional
religious beliefs passed down through my family and culture. This though,
changed once I encountered Camus and Seneca. I believe, as the existentialists
do, that life is inherently meaningless. And in this life, I believe that
meaning will not be found, and that I cannot instill myself, any meaning into
it, as well as that since this is a meaningless existence, nothing in it truly
matters. But, whereas the Nihilist for example, would say this, and therefore
simply never care for anything, I favor the prospect of accepting this absurd
reality, and therefore embracing it. This being the case, let it be known that
I do not believe that there is a correct way to go about doing so. I do not
believe that a hedonistic response is the only answer to this, nor do I believe
that a nihilist response is the only answer. But personally, I have used the
works of the stoics to shape my own response. Stoicism holds that virtue is
above all else, the ideal. To live in an endless pursuit of virtue, is a life
well-lived. Now, do I believe virtue matters? This is where I start to either
doubt myself or lose all sense of what I thought was a solid train of thought.
If nothing matters, but I choose to live virtuously due to the fact that it
provides me with a challenge, a sense of peace, and what I feel provides others
with positivity, even if they share opposing beliefs, am I therefore acknowledging
that virtue matters? Or can I simply maintain that nothing matters, and that I
am simply choosing to follow the stoic path simply because I have the freedom
to do so and am therefore choosing this path among many others. I guess the
question here, is if I am stating that nothing matters, can my choice to adhere
to certain teachings, not attribute importance to them? I believe so. </p>

<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; I am therefore supporting the claim that
nothing matters, on the basis of the beliefs that life is inherently
meaningless, there is no afterlife, and that individuals possess an absolute
will in their own agency. This being said, I do not necessarily infer that because
nothing matters, there is necessarily a correct, or logical result of that
claim. I believe, as the existentialists do, that because that is the case,
embrace free will and your own freedom as an agent in existence, choosing to go
and think where and what you please henceforth. </p>
