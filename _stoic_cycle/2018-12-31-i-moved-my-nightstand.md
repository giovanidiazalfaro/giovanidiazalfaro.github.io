<p>     This Monday morning my alarm sounded, just as it does every morning.  And just as routinely as it sounded, I snoozed it. This tiny, pleasurable act is one of the most common fallible acts many commit on a daily basis. And when I finally woke up and got out of bed hours later, I thought about this. </p>

<p>     A few thoughts crossed my mind when I sat down and considered why I allow myself to put off my responsibilities, and make a decision that I clearly know is more detrimental than beneficial. </p>

<p>One: Quite simply, it's easier to avoid than it is to confront. </p>

<p>Two: I set myself up for this by going to bed unnecessarily late.</p>

<p>Three: My phone is within arm's reach from where I sleep. </p>

<p>     Now going through these, there's plenty to be said. Considering the first thought, it's something largely pervasive in a more general sense as well. No matter what the matter of concern, it is usually much easier to put off thinking about it or working through it than it is to confront the issue or dilemma. This can be said just as easily for matters such as signing divorce papers as it could be for smaller issues such as getting an oil change, or turning off an alarm and getting up. These temptations to ignore, and the detrimentally false pleasures we derive from doing so are some of the greatest enemies to progress and productivity. </p>

<p>     It is also just as detrimentally pleasurable to stay up late for some reason. I spent my evening off of work doing a mix of reading, cooking, TED Talk watching, and cleaning. I was quite content with that, and at around 11ish I thought to myself: "The gym is closed tomorrow morning, but I should get to bed anyway and still wake up early to get some things done and get ahead." Thirty minutes later, I was on the couch playing Super Smash Bros. I was doing this while knowing I have no reason to be doing so, and should be asleep in bed. Now why I, or anyone else does this I don't quite have an explanation for. Is it because it provides me with an excuse for not waking up early? "Sorry, I went to bed late and wanted to make sure I got my rest in regardless." Or does it go back to the ease of ignorance described prior? This is something which I most definitely plan on exploring further in the future.</p>

<p>     The one thought that held the most value to me though, and kept recurring, was that of the proximity of my phone to me during the night. The purpose of the alarm, is to wake me and get me up and out of bed so I can start my day, right? So why, if this is the case, do I allow myself such an easy way to oppose such a purpose? If I were to simply keep my phone somewhere out of arm's reach, but still in my bedroom, I would be forced to get up and out of bed, ensuring that I fulfill at least one of the two intended purposes of the alarm that I've stated. And so, I moved my nightstand. Not to the other side of my room, or some other unnecessary distance, but simply from beside my bed, to a corner a few feet further, out of arm's reach. I reorganized it, placing my lamp, wireless, charger, water bottle, TV remote, and books back on top, and did so in a very particular manner. My goal is to make sure that to read, watch TV, or use my phone from bed -- I have to get out of bed, and grab those items. To stop doing any of those actions-- I now also have to get out of bed and place them back. But most importantly, to sleep -- I have to place all of those items back on the table, out of arm's reach. And to turn off that alarm in the morning, I have to get up and out of bed. Eventually, depending on the results of this minor change, I'm considering removing the phone from the bedroom completely during the night. But for now, we'll see how this goes. </p>
