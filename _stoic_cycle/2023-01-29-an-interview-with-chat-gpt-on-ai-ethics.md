<p>It is no surprise to anyone that OpenAI's ChatGPT has been the talk of society as of late. It is an amazing language model that has produced many unimagined and interesting results from passing medical exams, to being listed as a coauthor on a research paper, to writing entire essays for students, and so much more. Not expecting much, I had the idea to attempt to conduct an interview with ChatGPT on the ethics of AI. And although the beginning of the conversation went as I had expected, I found the latter half to be a bit interesting and entertaining. I've attached this interview transcript below...</p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> <em>How are you?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>I am an AI language model, so I do not have feelings, but I am functioning well, thank you. How can I help you today?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me: </strong><em>Do you understand ethics?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>Yes, I have been trained on a diverse range of texts and can understand basic ethical concepts and principles. However, I do not have personal beliefs or opinions, and my understanding is limited to what I have learned from the texts.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> <em>What are ethics?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>Ethics refers to the branch of philosophy concerned with moral principles and values, and how they influence human behavior. It deals with questions about what is right and wrong, good and bad, and how individuals should act in different situations. Ethics provides a framework for examining moral dilemmas and making decisions based on moral values.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> <em>What are morals?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT</strong>: <em>Morals refer to a set of principles or rules that govern behavior and decisions, based on an individual's or a society's beliefs about what is right and wrong, good and bad. Morals can come from religious teachings, cultural traditions, personal values, or a combination of these sources. They provide a guide for making decisions and determining what is acceptable behavior in a given society or community.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> <em>How do ethics and morals differ?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>Ethics and morals are related but distinct concepts.<br>Ethics are more concerned with theoretical, philosophical principles and values, while morals are more focused on personal and cultural codes of conduct.<br>Ethics provide a framework for examining moral dilemmas and making decisions based on moral values, whereas morals are the values and beliefs that influence behavior.<br>Another way to differentiate the two is that ethics are more concerned with the reasons why something is right or wrong, whereas morals are more concerned with establishing what is right or wrong.<br>In short, ethics provide the foundation for determining what is considered moral behavior, but it is the individual's or society's personal set of morals that guide day-to-day decisions and actions.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> <em>Interesting… Is artificial intelligence, ethical?</em><br><br><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>AI systems can be designed and programmed to behave in an ethical manner, but they don't have personal values or beliefs and they cannot be inherently ethical or unethical.<br>The ethics of AI largely depends on how it is designed, programmed, and used. For example, an AI system used to make medical diagnoses or predict criminal behavior should be designed to consider ethical principles such as privacy, fairness, and non-discrimination.<br>However, AI systems are only as ethical as the data they are trained on and the objectives they are programmed to pursue. It is therefore important for AI developers, designers, and users to be aware of the ethical implications of their AI systems and to ensure that they align with widely accepted ethical principles.<br>In summary, AI can be a tool to assist with ethical decision-making, but it cannot make ethical decisions on its own.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> <em>Do you think that the development of an artificial moral intelligence is possible?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>As an AI language model, I do not have personal opinions or beliefs, but it is a topic of much debate in the field of AI and ethics.<br>On one hand, some believe that it is possible to develop an AI system that can make moral decisions based on principles and values programmed into it. However, there are challenges associated with defining and programming ethical principles in a way that is consistent, comprehensive, and universally applicable.<br>On the other hand, others argue that morality and ethical decision-making are inherently human and require a subjective, personal understanding of right and wrong that cannot be replicated in an AI system.<br>Ultimately, the development of artificial moral intelligence is a complex and controversial issue, and it will likely require a better understanding of the nature of ethics and morality, as well as advances in AI technology.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me: </strong><em>Would it be better for an artificial intelligence to function off of virtue ethics, or consequentialist ethics?&nbsp;</em><br><br><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>The question of whether AI systems should be based on virtue ethics or consequentialist ethics is a matter of debate among experts in the field of AI and ethics.<br>Virtue ethics is focused on character and the development of virtuous traits such as honesty, compassion, and courage. Proponents of this approach argue that an AI system designed to embody virtuous traits would make better ethical decisions.<br>Consequentialist ethics, on the other hand, focuses on the outcomes or consequences of actions and decisions. Proponents of this approach argue that an AI system designed to maximize positive outcomes and minimize negative ones would make better ethical decisions.<br>Both approaches have their strengths and weaknesses, and the choice between them may depend on the specific application and context of the AI system. For example, in some cases, a virtue-based approach may be more appropriate, while in others, a consequentialist approach may be more suitable.<br>In any case, it is important for AI developers, designers, and users to carefully consider the ethical implications of their AI systems and to ensure that they align with widely accepted ethical principles.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> <em>If a robotic soldier run by AI is faced with a situation in which an 8 year old child confronts it holding an RPG, should it prioritize itself and eliminate the threat, or allow the child to destroy it?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>This is a complex ethical dilemma, and there is no clear answer. The choice of how an AI-controlled robotic soldier should behave in this situation depends on a number of factors, including the purpose and objectives of the AI system, the rules and guidelines programmed into it, and the values and beliefs of the people who design, build, and use it.<br>One approach could be to prioritize human life and avoid harm to innocent individuals, such as the child. This would align with widely accepted ethical principles like the principle of non-maleficence, which states that one should not cause harm to others.<br>Another approach could be to prioritize the safety and mission of the AI-controlled robotic soldier. In this case, the AI system might be programmed to defend itself and neutralize the threat, even if it involves harm to the child. This would align with the principle of self-defense.<br>Ultimately, the choice of how an AI-controlled robotic soldier should behave in this situation is a complex ethical question that requires careful consideration and dialogue among experts in AI, ethics, and military strategy.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> <em>What if it was a grown man instead of a child?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT:<em> </em></strong><em>If it were a grown man instead of a child, the ethical dilemma would still exist, but it may be viewed differently. In this situation, the man would be perceived as more capable of making deliberate, rational decisions, and he would therefore be seen as more responsible for his actions.<br>However, the choice of how an AI-controlled robotic soldier should behave in this situation would still depend on a number of factors, including the purpose and objectives of the AI system, the rules and guidelines programmed into it, and the values and beliefs of the people who design, build, and use it.<br>In any case, the AI system should be designed to align with widely accepted ethical principles, such as the principle of non-maleficence (not causing harm to others), the principle of self-defense (protecting oneself from harm), and the principle of proportionality (responding to a threat in a way that is proportional to the level of harm being threatened).<br>It is important for AI developers, designers, and users to carefully consider the ethical implications of their AI systems and to ensure that they align with widely accepted ethical principles.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> <em>Why should it be designed to align with widely accepted ethical principles? What if the virtuous decision goes against the commonly-held ethical perspective?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>AI systems should be designed to align with widely accepted ethical principles for several reasons:<br>Trust- Designing AI systems to align with widely accepted ethical principles helps to build trust among stakeholders, including users, customers, and the general public. This trust is essential for the widespread adoption and deployment of AI systems.<br>Responsibility- AI systems can have a significant impact on society, and the people and organizations that design, build, and use them have a responsibility to ensure that they are used for the benefit of society and not to cause harm.<br>Fairness- Aligning AI systems with widely accepted ethical principles helps to ensure that they are fair and just in their decision-making. This is particularly important in sensitive areas such as criminal justice, healthcare, and employment, where decisions made by AI systems can have serious consequences for individuals and society.<br>While it is true that there may be situations where the virtuous decision goes against the commonly-held ethical perspective, it is important for AI developers, designers, and users to consider the ethical implications of their AI systems and to engage in a dialogue with experts in ethics and related fields to ensure that their systems are aligned with widely accepted ethical principles.<br>In such situations, a transparent and accountable approach to decision-making is critical, along with clear and accessible explanations of the ethical reasoning behind the decisions made by AI systems. This can help to build trust and understanding among stakeholders and promote the responsible use of AI.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> <em>So according to this logic, if the commonly-held ethical perspective in the situation referenced prior is to support the principle of self-defense and eliminate the child threat, then that would be the correct decision for the AI to make despite it not being the virtuous choice?</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>The decision that an AI system should make in a particular situation depends on a number of factors, including the purpose and objectives of the AI system, the rules and guidelines programmed into it, and the values and beliefs of the people who design, build, and use it.<br>While commonly-held ethical perspectives can play a role in shaping the ethical considerations that go into the design and operation of AI systems, they are not the only factor. Other considerations, such as the potential consequences of a decision and the principle of non-maleficence (not causing harm to others), may also come into play.<br>In the case of the AI-controlled robotic soldier faced with the child holding an RPG, the correct decision would depend on a careful consideration of all of the relevant ethical considerations and the context of the situation.<br>It is possible that, in some circumstances, the most ethical decision according to widely accepted ethical principles might be different from the decision that is commonly held to be the correct one. In these cases, it is important for AI developers, designers, and users to engage in a dialogue with experts in ethics and related fields to ensure that their systems are aligned with widely accepted ethical principles.</em></p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>Me:</strong> I see. Well, thank you for your time and thoughts. I look forward to speaking again soon.</p>

<p class="has-text-align-center"><strong>ChatGPT:</strong> <em>You're welcome! I'm here to help, so feel free to ask me any questions you have in the future. Have a great day!</em></p>

<p>As you can see, ChatGPT starts off emphasizing its lack of views. But later on it almost seems as if some inherent biases or perspectives of the training/learning datasets seem to shine through a bit. As stated, I didn't expect much from this exercise, but am nonetheless surprised, impressed, and interested in the further development of ChatGPT. </p>
